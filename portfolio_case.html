OVERVIEW

Having experienced being a Freelancer in both the Education and UX Design fields, I understand the need to be a well-rounded professional who can take different roles.

But what about hired UX Designers?

From quick browsing in Forums, I noticed that the vast majority of UX designers that replied, stated they have basic coding skills. That pushed me to learn practical coding skills, allowing me to communicate efficiently with developers.

PROBLEM:

Although I took a crash course in Java, it is very different then front-end languages which I had no knowledge of. On top of that. The Career Foundry course is mostly text based, which makes it harder to learn then video, for my taste.

SOLUTION:

To accompany my Career Foundry task-oriented course I took other deep-dive HTML, CSS, and JavaScript courses.
